Complaint Escalation Process

What is a Complaint?

A complaint is an expression of dissatisfaction or concern regarding a particular situation, service, or product. In an educational context, complaints may arise from students, parents, or staff about issues such as unfair treatment, inadequate resources, or unaddressed grievances.

Purpose of the Complaint Escalation Process

The complaint escalation process is designed to ensure that all concerns are addressed systematically and fairly. It involves several levels of approval, ensuring that complaints are evaluated and resolved by appropriate authorities.

Steps in the Escalation Process

1. **Class Teacher Approval**: The initial stage where the class teacher reviews the complaint and determines its validity.

2. **Head of Department Approval**: If the complaint is upheld, it is escalated to the head of the department for further assessment.

3. **Principal Approval**: The final stage involves the principal, who makes the ultimate decision on the complaint.
